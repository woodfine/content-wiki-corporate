Freely Transferable Investment Units

## 12 Capital Framework & Domestic Alignment

Capital Framework & Domestic Alignment

The platform is built upon a strict separation of debt and equity.

## 15 Principal Alignment & Preserved Fees

Principal Alignment & Preserved Fees

Our Direct-Hold Solutions restructure the classic 2/20 model by having the Developer take compensation in common equity as carry.
This discipline enforces a strict 1:1 alignment with investors, with physical allocations held in trust until initial capital is fully returned.
Because developer profit is not extracted as cash, it becomes retained equity on the investment vehicle's balance sheet, acting as a structural reserve.
This allows for variable management fees to be replaced with a single fixed annual fee based on the gross funded value, resulting in preserved fees that remain fully available to compensate the independent Asset Managers orchestrating the external SPVs.

## 16 Perpetual Equity & Debt Execution

The Narrow Bank Financial Model

This model of perpetual equity allows the construction of the initial buildings—yielding unlevered square feet—with no requirement for pre-leasing or construction loans.
Once cash flow is stabilized, the investment vehicle issues its own first secured mortgage debentures for future development. These debentures are 100% collateralized directly by the physical Woodfine Buildings. Consequently, the Direct-Hold Solution carries no abstract corporate debt; all liabilities are strictly ring-fenced to the real estate. In the event of a foreclosure, the transfer of the underlying asset extinguishes the debt entirely, guaranteeing that investors will never face capital calls to cover borrowing liabilities.
During this Leverage Cycle, the debt-to-asset value reaches no more than ~60%.
As the portfolio enters the Management Phase, a mandated 10% of distributable income is utilized to systematically redeem this debt.
The Loan-to-Value ratio continuously decreases over time, driven organically by income cash flow rather than relying on external refinancing activities.
