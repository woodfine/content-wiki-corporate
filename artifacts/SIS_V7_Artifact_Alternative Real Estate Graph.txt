**Legacy Joint Ventures vs. Direct-Hold Solutions**

As the graph below illustrates, with the same amount of initial capital, the Direct-Hold Solution develops fewer unlevered square feet at the beginning of the cycle, but is able to develop over 40% more square feet by the end of the cycle as compared to legacy joint venture financing.

Even though the debt-to-equity ratio reaches roughly 4.5 times, the debt-to-asset-value ratio reaches no more than 60% during the leverage cycle and remains stable at 50% LTV during the management phase (6.25 cap).

This is a direct result of the Principal Manager taking compensation in common equity. Because developer profit is not extracted as cash, it becomes Retained Equity on the investment vehicle's balance sheet, acting as a Structural Reserve until the maximum leverage ratios are achieved and the development phase is completed—a structural superiority which results in a different outcome for investors.
