**Executive Summary: Mix-of-Use Environment, Professional Ecosystem, and Co-location at Power Centres**

The Woodfine Direct-Hold Solution develops Institutional-Grade Regional Real Estate in a Mix-of-Use environment. This environment is structured around Woodfine Buildings, which include Professional Centres, Suburban Offices, Retail Select Buildings, and Tech Industrial Buildings. These assets serve as a dedicated office component within established commercial areas, distinct from general mixed-use properties.

**Co-location at Power Centres**
The development strategy targets locations on or adjacent to existing Power Centres. A Power Centre is defined as a large aggregation of retail stores, typically anchored by large box-store commercial retail tenants. This strategy follows the expansion patterns of National Retailers, such as Walmart, Costco, and Home Depot, which conduct demographic research for co-location within 1 to 3 kilometers of each other. This positioning leverages the "halo effect" of retail resilience, driven by consistent foot traffic from necessity-based and grocery-anchored retail. Investor demand for such retail centres is present due to the cash flow stability they offer during economic volatility.

Locating Woodfine Buildings at Power Centres introduces a complementary user base. Office tenants generate consistent weekday traffic, balancing existing retail patterns and contributing to the revitalization of Power Centres. The built environment across these Power Centres exhibits uniformity, regardless of geographic jurisdiction.

**Curated Professional Ecosystem**
The Mix-of-Use environment establishes a curated professional ecosystem designed for Academic, Business, Civic, Laboratory, and Medical users. The tenant mix targets 30% to 50% government offices, with local law, accounting, and health services occupying the remainder. Professionals and government agencies seek high-quality infrastructure located where staff and clients conduct daily activities.

Woodfine Buildings are engineered to meet Dual-Mandate Building Certifications, combining baseline criteria for Environmental Performance with standards for Human-Centric Wellness. This approach attracts high-covenant National Tenants and provides premium real estate. The structural design integrates elements such as Human-Centric Wellness and Human Scale, ensuring functional and efficient leaseholds. Features like European Lighting Directives and Swiss Air Exchange Requirements contribute to a mathematically demonstrable 30% reduction in operating costs for office tenants. Parking ratios are maintained at a minimum of 3:1000, with a target of 4:1000, to accommodate the operational demands of National Tenants. This strategic co-tenancy ensures Woodfine Buildings are supported by resilient economic drivers.
